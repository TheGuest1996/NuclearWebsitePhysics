An Overview and Insight into Nuclear Fusion and Fission



Social media use among teenagers is widespread, with many spending hours scrolling through platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and X (formerly Twitter). Concerns have grown over the potential negative impacts of prolonged social media use on young people’s mental health and well-being. While some studies highlight the positive aspects of social media, such as fostering connections, others point to risks lke social stigma, anxiety, and low self-esteem.
The data collected was from two different sources, one of which was from Generation Survey and the other was from Pew Research Center. The data gathered from Generation Survey was available through their website. It is a large survey conducted in 2024 to explore the impact of social media use on the well-being of young people that targeted 3,785 Year 10 students across 340 Australian schools.
The data gathered from Pew Research Center was available online. The sample consisted of 1,316 U.S. teens aged 13 to 17, along with one parent from each teen’s household in 2022.
