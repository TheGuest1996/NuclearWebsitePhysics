What are the major risks and benefits associated with nuclear energy use in modern society, and how do these influence energy policy?
Benefits: Nuclear energy itself does not emit carbon dioxide and other pollutants/GHGs
Risks: Radioactive waste disposal and storage Non-renewable Water-intensive (used for cooling and turning steam turbines)

How do sociocultural and political attitudes toward nuclear energy differ across countries, and what factors shape these perspectives (including legal views)?
Historical Experience with Nuclear Events Germany Anti-nuclear stance Deeply affected by Chernobyl (1986) and Fukushima (2011). Led to strong public opposition and a political movement that pushed the Energiewende (energy transition). Result: Decision to phase out all nuclear power, despite climate concerns.

France Pro-nuclear tradition Adopted nuclear early after the 1970s oil crisis to ensure energy independence. ~70% of electricity comes from nuclear. Nuclear is seen as a source of national pride and low-carbon energy.

Japan Mixed to anti-nuclear since 2011 Public support dropped sharply after Fukushima. Nuclear restarts since then have been controversial and slow. The legacy of Hiroshima and Nagasaki also shapes cultural sensitivity to radiation.
